Cab Calloway

Singer and Bandleader - "Hi De Hi De Ho!"

9 Fascinating Facts

  1. Cab Calloway was born on Christmas Day in 1907.
  2. Louis Armstrong taught him the art of 'scat singing' - the use of nonsensical sounds to improvise melodies. The phrase 'Hi de hi de ho' was his most famous.
  3. In 1930 Cab got a gig at Harlem's famous Cotton club. He became a regular performer there as the bandleader of Cab Calloway and his Orchestra.
  4. He hit the big time in 1931 with "Minnie the Moocher". It was a No.1 song that sold more than a million copies.
  5. The composer George Gershwin, wrote the character Sportin' Life for him. From 1952 Cab spent two years in that role in the musical 'Porgy and Bess'.
  6. He made appearances on the TV show 'Sesame Street', in Janet Jackson's "Alright" music video and in the 'Blues Brothers' film in 1980.
  7. He wrote his autobiogaphy 'Of Minnie the Moocher and Me' (1976).
  8. In 1993 Prseident Bill Clinton presented Cab with a National Medal of the Arts.
  9. Cab Calloway died on 18 November 1994 at the age of 86.
